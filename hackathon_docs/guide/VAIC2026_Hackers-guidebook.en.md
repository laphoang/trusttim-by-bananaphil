## Chapter 1: Overview

### 1.1 Welcome Message from the Organizing Committee of Vietnam AI Innovation Challenge 2026

Welcome to Vietnam AI Innovation Challenge 2026 – VAIC 2026!

The competition will take place from July 17–19, 2026 at the National Innovation Center (NIC), Hoa Lac. VAIC 2026 is organized by the National Innovation Center (NIC), Meta Corporation, the AI for Vietnam Foundation, and Duy Tan University.

The program is also accompanied by important partners in the AI, technology, education, and media ecosystem, including:

- **Accompanying units:** National Institute of Digital Technology and Digital Transformation, AI Singapore, STEAM for Vietnam, Vietnam AI Stars
- **Sponsoring unit:** FPT AI Factory

- **Media partners:** Hub Network, Dinosaur, Nghien AI

For the first time in history, Vietnam is entering a major technology revolution at nearly the same starting point as the rest of the world. The rapid development of artificial intelligence is not only creating new tools, but also opening opportunities for Vietnam to participate more deeply in shaping the future of the digital economy, national competitiveness, and a new generation of technology products.

However, to turn that opportunity into a real advantage, Vietnam needs to close the gap between businesses' practical needs and the implementation capacity of the young tech community. Many businesses are searching for AI solutions that can be applied to real problems, while talented young people need a sufficiently challenging environment to learn, collide with real issues, build products, and grow through hands-on experience.

VAIC 2026 was created to connect these two needs. The competition offers a playground where real-world business problems meet the creativity, technical skill, and dedication of Vietnam's young generation. Through a proprietary technology platform, a system of practical challenges, and the companionship of a network of experts, mentors, and partners at home and abroad, VAIC 2026 is not just a hackathon, but an acceleration environment for turning bold ideas into workable prototypes.

Under the guiding message **"TRAIN'SFORMATION,"** VAIC 2026 brings together young talents from all over the country on a shared journey. Over 48 hours, teams will learn fast, experiment fast, build fast, and present AI solutions aimed at real-world impact. This is not just a place to compete, but also a place where each contestant helps build the foundation for Vietnam's AI ecosystem in the coming decade.

This guidebook will accompany you throughout your VAIC 2026 journey, providing important information about the schedule, regulations, ways of working, evaluation criteria, submission format, and other necessary notes.

Get ready to step into the TRAIN'SFORMATION journey — where you and your teammates accelerate, build products, and help create real impact for Vietnam's AI ecosystem.

### 1.3. General Introduction to VAIC 2026

#### 1.3.1. Objectives

VAIC 2026 is organized with the goal of creating a real-world AI playground, where young talents have the opportunity to access real business problems, work in a high-intensity environment, and build prototypes capable of creating real-world impact.

The competition aims to shorten the gap between ideas and implementation. Rather than simply stopping at presenting a concept, teams will be challenged to turn an idea into a demonstrable prototype with clear product logic and the potential to develop further after the competition.

Through VAIC 2026, the organizing committee hopes to:

- Promote the spirit of applying AI to solve real-world problems for businesses and society.
- Create an environment for young people to learn, collide with challenges, and grow through the experience of building a product in 48 hours.
- Connect contestants with mentors, experts, businesses, investors, and the tech community.
- Contribute to forming a generation of Vietnamese AI builders with creative capacity, product thinking, and real-world implementation ability.

VAIC 2026 is not only looking for the best-performing teams, but also aims to build a community of young people who dare to think, dare to act, and are ready to contribute to the future of Vietnam's AI ecosystem.

#### 1.3.2. Who Is This Competition For?

VAIC 2026 is for young people interested in AI, technology, product, design, business, and innovation. Contestants may come from many different backgrounds, such as programming, data science, design, business administration, research, communications, or other fields relevant to the challenge topics.

**On age:** The competition has no age limit. However, contestants under 18 need to meet one of the following two conditions:

- Have written consent from a parent or legal guardian (using the Organizing Committee's form); or
- Be accompanied by a parent/guardian throughout the event.

A team can include many different roles, for example:

- Members in charge of engineering, AI, data, or backend/frontend.
- Members in charge of product, user analysis, and solution direction.
- Members in charge of interface design, user experience, or prototyping.
- Members in charge of business strategy, deployment models, and the pitch.

> Contestants are not required to be AI experts to participate. What matters is a spirit of learning, the ability to work as a team, problem-solving thinking, and a readiness to build products in a real-world, high-intensity environment.

## Chapter 2: Rules and Challenge Statements

### 2.1. Overview of Competition Rules

Vietnam AI Innovation Challenge 2026 – VAIC 2026 is a 48-hour AI hackathon competition, where teams work together to develop technology solutions to solve real-world problems from businesses, organizations, and the innovation ecosystem.

At VAIC 2026, teams will choose 1 of 8 challenge themes. Each theme represents a group of problems with the potential for AI application to create practical impact, ranging from operational optimization, enhancing user experience, and supporting decision-making, to developing new product, service, and business models.

The competition does not only evaluate teams based on coding ability or the level of technical completeness, but also comprehensively considers many factors: the ability to correctly understand the problem, product thinking, the level of creativity, user experience, real-world application potential, and how the team conveys the value of the solution.

Through VAIC 2026, teams are challenged not only to create a good idea, but also to turn that idea into a demonstrable prototype with clear implementation logic and the potential for further development after the competition.

**8 Challenge Themes:**

1. **Banking & Finance** — Applying AI to reshape the finance industry, from operational automation and risk management to enhancing customer experience.
2. **Health & Wellness** — Applying AI in healthcare: supporting diagnosis, optimizing operations, and improving the quality of medical services.
3. **Education & Training** — Personalizing learning, optimizing educational resources, and supporting career development guidance for learners.
4. **Disaster Prevention** — Forecasting, modeling, and building response solutions — resilience against natural disasters.
5. **Innovation** — Developing new products, improving processes, and applying innovative methods.
6. **SME Productivity** — Building AI agents that revolutionize operations for small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs).
7. **Smart Government** — Solutions for smart cities and digital public services.
8. **Agriculture** — Optimizing supply chains, precision farming, and adapting to climate change.

> Detailed information for each challenge topic, including context, output requirements, supporting resources, and specific evaluation criteria, will be announced at 11:00 AM on July 17, 2026.

### 2.2. Competition Format

The competition takes place as an in-person hackathon. Over 48 hours, teams will work at the competition venue, receive support from mentors, participate in scheduled activities, and complete their product to submit on time.

**Basic process (in-person hackathon, over 48 hours):**

1. **Receive challenge information:** Teams are introduced to the groups of challenge topics, problem context, output requirements, and evaluation criteria. (Sample challenge)
2. **Choose a challenge:** Each team selects a challenge topic that fits their capabilities, product direction, and interests.
3. **Develop a solution:** Teams analyze the problem, identify the target users, build an approach, develop a prototype, and prepare the demo.
4. **Mentoring and product refinement:** During the competition, teams can receive feedback from mentors to adjust direction, improve the product, and prepare their presentation. (Read more in the Genius Station section.)
5. **Submission and presentation:** Teams submit 5 documents according to the list below, and the Top 10 present their products before the judging panel:
   - Presentation slides
   - Demo video (≤ 5 min)
   - GitHub repository (public)
   - Live deployed URL
   - Project description

### 2.3. Judging Panel Composition

The VAIC 2026 judging panel is built to be multi-dimensional, ensuring that entries are evaluated not only from a technical perspective, but also from the perspectives of industry, users, implementation feasibility, and post-competition development potential. Depending on the challenge theme, the judging panel may include:

1. **Domain Expert** — An expert with deep understanding of the field or industry related to the challenge theme. The Domain Expert helps evaluate how well the solution fits the real-world context, market needs, operational characteristics, and the core issues the business or organization is facing.

2. **Technical Judge** — An expert or practitioner in the AI/ML field, capable of evaluating system architecture, how AI is integrated, the quality of technical implementation, the operability of the prototype, and the soundness of technology choices.

3. **Non-tech Industry Judge** — A representative of the end-user perspective or application departments within a business, such as HR, accounting, operations, customer service, or other related departments. This role helps ensure the solution is not only technically correct, but also understandable, useful, suited to real-world usage contexts, and likely to be adopted by users.

4. **Senior Judge** — During Demo Day, the judging panel may include Senior Judges who are senior executives, founders, investors, fund representatives, or AI lab leaders. Senior Judges contribute a strategic perspective on development potential, scalability, market value, and the long-term impact of the solution.

Combining multiple perspectives on the judging panel helps VAIC 2026 evaluate teams comprehensively: from technical quality and depth of expertise to user experience and the ability to apply the solution in practice and develop it into a valuable product.

## Chapter 3: Teams and Team Formation

### 3.1. Team Formation Rules and Eligibility Requirements

- Each team has 2–6 members. Individual entries are not accepted.
- Eligible contestants are those who have completed the application process and been confirmed to participate.
- Teams need to confirm their team name, member names, and chosen track/theme before 10:00 AM on July 17, 2026.
- Each team chooses 1 of 8 challenge themes and competes throughout the 48 hours based on that chosen theme.

### 3.2. Ways to Form a Team

**PATH A — Already Have a Team**
The team leader creates a team profile on the Platform and invites members by name or email. The team profile can include the team name, a short description, topics of interest, and any missing roles.

**PATH B — Contestants Without a Team**
Contestants without a team will be placed into the Solo Hacker Pool. The Platform may suggest team matches based on skills, roles, and topics of interest.

### 3.3. General Technical Requirements

- The product must be built in the spirit of AI-Native.
- The entire core product/code must be developed within the 48 hours of the competition.
- AI must play a clear role in the solution, not merely a supporting element.
- Teams must complete all required submission items:
  - Presentation slides
  - Demo video no longer than 5 minutes
  - Public GitHub repository
  - Live deployed URL
  - AI collaboration log

### 3.4. Core Values Teams Should Keep in Mind

**100% AI-Native Oath**
An expert with deep understanding of the field or industry related to the challenge theme. The Domain Expert helps evaluate how well the solution fits the real-world context, market needs, operational characteristics, and the core issues the business or organization is facing.

**"Ends in a Startup" Philosophy**
VAIC 2026 is not only focused on winning prizes, but also encourages teams to build solutions that can continue to develop into a startup, a corporate pilot, or a product with real-world impact.

**Genius Station**
During the 48-hour competition, teams can receive support from Genius Station — a group of 15–20 AI, technical, and product experts. The experts will provide support in shifts and can be reached via the Online Emergency Call channel when needed.

## Chapter 4: Logistics and Safety Regulations

### 4.1. Participation Costs for VAIC 2026

Applications: Free of charge to submit an application.

### 4.2 Logistics Support

Throughout VAIC 2026, the Organizing Committee will support basic logistical needs so that teams can focus as much as possible on product development. Support items include:

- **Rest areas at the venue:** Contestants can take short breaks in areas set up within the venue. Please look after your own personal belongings.
- **Food:** The Organizing Committee provides main meals according to the event schedule.
- **Drinking water:** Drinking water is provided at the competition area and at common support points.
- **Basic medical/support area:** In case of an emergency, contestants can contact volunteers or the Organizing Committee at the information area.

Contestants are encouraged to prepare additional necessary personal items such as a sleeping bag, water bottle, jacket, personal hygiene items, personal medication, laptop/phone chargers, and other equipment needed for working over the 48 hours.

### 4.3. Code of Conduct and Technology Ethics

All contestants must adhere to the following principles:

- Respect teammates, mentors, judges, volunteers, the Organizing Committee, and the event community.
- Do not copy or plagiarize the products, work, source code, or ideas of other teams/third parties without proper authorization or attribution.
- Data used in the project, e.g. medical, financial, or personal data, must be handled in accordance with proper security, privacy, and data ethics regulations.
- Do not use sensitive data, personal data, or illegally collected data.
- Violations of the AI-Native Oath, or fraudulent acts such as submitting a pre-existing product, falsifying records, or faking the development process, may lead to the team being disqualified from prize consideration.

### 4.4. Prohibited Items in the Competition Area

Do not bring the following items into the competition area without permission from the Organizing Committee:

- Flammable/explosive substances or hazardous chemicals.
- Sharp objects or large metal objects.
- Signal-jamming devices, or unlicensed transceiver devices that could affect the event's network infrastructure.
- Alcoholic beverages, illegal stimulants.
- Drones or flying devices.

The Organizing Committee reserves the right to inspect, require the deposit of, or refuse items that pose a risk to the safety and operation of the event.
