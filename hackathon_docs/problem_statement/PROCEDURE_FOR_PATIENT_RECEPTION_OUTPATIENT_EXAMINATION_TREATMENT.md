## I. PURPOSE

To establish a standardized reception and outpatient examination/treatment procedure for all categories of patients coming to the Voluntary (Self-Pay) Outpatient Department of Hanoi Heart Hospital, so that patients can be examined quickly, conveniently, and safely.

## II. SCOPE OF APPLICATION

Applies to all categories of patients coming for outpatient examination and treatment at the Voluntary (Self-Pay) Area 1, Facility 1.

## III. REFERENCE DOCUMENTS

Hospital Regulations — Medical Publishing House, 1997.

## V. PROCEDURE CONTENT

### 5.1. Reception and outpatient examination/treatment flow — Voluntary (Self-Pay) Area 1, Facility 1, Hanoi Heart Hospital

| Responsibility | Process steps | Description / Related documents |
|---|---|---|
| **Customer Care staff — Social Work Department** | Receive Voluntary Area 1 appointment bookings and advise on the examination process via phone, website, and Fanpage | Patients access appointment information via the follow-up appointment slip, the medical record cover, and official online information pages to book an appointment and choose a doctor at Voluntary Area 1. Patients who book are advised and given their appointment information: booking code (reception queue number), examination date, and doctor (if applicable). Patients who did not book take a queue number directly from the automatic ticket kiosk in Voluntary Area 1. |
| **Consultation & Reception staff — Guides (HDV)** | Patient takes a queue number for reception | - Patients who already booked go directly to the consultation counter reserved for pre-booked patients and inform staff of their booking details to complete registration.<br>- Patients without a prior booking take a queue number from the automatic kiosk and wait to be called in order.<br>- Guides (HDV) direct patients to the ticket area and maintain order in the waiting area. |
| **Consultation & Reception staff** | Receive registration information | Patients registering for examination at Voluntary Area 1 fall into these categories: with health insurance (BHYT) and without; follow-up visit and new visit.<br>- First-time patients fill in their information on the registration form.<br>- BHYT patients are guided to present their hospital-transfer letter, follow-up appointment slip, and BHYT card (if the physical BHYT card is not available, the BHYT image on the VssID app or a chip-based national ID with integrated BHYT may be used instead), national ID or another photo ID, and copies of the necessary documents.<br>- Patients with a new transfer letter are guided to sign the Commitment Form agreeing to pay the difference in medical service fees for examination and treatment at Hanoi Heart Hospital.<br>- BHYT patients are advised to prepare the necessary documents in advance to make it easier for accounting staff to check and process.<br>- All registering patients are asked to present their national ID or another photo ID (if available) for verification when providing registration information. If no ID is available, staff let the patient check the information written on the medical record cover after completing it.<br>- The medical record cover must be fully filled in with: full name, date of birth, address (at minimum down to commune/ward level), and phone number.<br>- The name of the doctor chosen by the patient is noted outside the record so the room-assignment staff can issue a queue number for that doctor's room.<br>- Patients in priority categories (per BVT Regulation No. 154 issued 12/01/2024) receive a "priority" stamp on their examination record.<br>- Patients are directed to the appropriate accounting counter (a counter for priority/pre-booked patients, and a counter for non-priority/walk-in patients). |
| **Accounting staff** | Collect fees and process BHYT (if applicable) | - BHYT patients present the necessary documents (transfer letter, follow-up appointment slip, BHYT card, VssID app, or chip-based ID with integrated BHYT).<br>- Accounting staff inform the patient of the examination fee and any BHYT co-payment difference, and collect payment.<br>- The patient is directed to the vital-signs, height, and weight station. |
| **Vital-signs staff** | Measure the patient's vital signs, height, and weight | At the vital-signs, height, and weight station:<br>- Staff measure vital signs, height, and weight, and record them on the patient reception form.<br>- Priority patients (per regulation) are served first.<br>- Staff follow the trained vital-signs measurement procedure.<br>- Abnormal cases are reported to a doctor or transferred to Emergency (per HD.25.01).<br>- Blood-pressure measurement or payment collection may be flexibly reordered depending on patient volume to avoid congestion. |
| **Nurses / Guides at the room-assignment desk — Result delivery** | Guide patients through diagnostic (CLS) tests and compile results before issuing a queue number for the doctor's conclusion | - Nurses issue a room queue number for patients not yet examined and direct them to the appropriate waiting area by queue number.<br>- For patients with diagnostic-test orders, the nurse explains the required steps:<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Step 1: staff write the queue number on the patient's test-order slips and payment receipts.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Step 2: staff check off the items the patient must complete and their locations on the instruction slip, so the patient understands the process and knows that all ordered tests must be finished before seeing the doctor for the conclusion. Staff return the payment receipt with the instruction slip attached to the patient.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Step 3: the guide (HDV) escorts the patient to complete the diagnostic tests: echocardiogram, abdominal/vascular ultrasound, X-ray, ECG, ABI, etc., and collects the results. Guides observe patients in the waiting area, help them find seats, and explain the process for subsequent tests. Guides at assigned stations deliver the patient's test results to the result-delivery desk for the nurse to check and clip into the medical record.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Step 4: the nurse reviews the diagnostic-test results. Abnormal cases are transferred to Emergency per HD.25.01. For other abnormal cases, the nurse consults the doctor or issues the patient a priority queue number to see the doctor. Complete cases are issued a queue number, have their record returned, and are directed to wait for the doctor's consultation room. |
| **Hallway guide (HDV)** | Direct patients and keep order in the waiting areas for consultation and diagnostic tests | - Patients are directed to sit in the waiting hall according to the room number of their registered doctor. Patients are told to watch the electronic display and listen for the number-call announcement to enter the room when called: the display shows a 4-digit queue number matching the patient's number (the first digit is the room number, the remaining digits are the sequence number).<br>- The guide is responsible for observing patients, relaying information reported by doctors to the result-delivery nurse's desk, reporting software or number-calling machine failures, patient questions beyond their ability to resolve, and any other abnormal situations. |
| **Doctors in examination rooms** | Examine the patient and prescribe treatment | Doctors in examination rooms:<br>- Call patients in order according to the number-calling software.<br>- Conduct the examination and prescribe using the clinical examination software system:<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ The doctor gathers complete information, including: epidemiology, medical history, and functional/physical symptoms.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Explains the patient's condition and the necessity of any diagnostic tests needed for diagnosis. The doctor selects and prints the diagnostic test order forms (if applicable).<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Explains the condition and prescribes necessary medication appropriate to the patient's condition per prescribing regulations (if applicable). When prescribing, the doctor must inform/consult the patient about the medication and its price to reach agreement.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Provides instructions on medication use, diet, exercise, and monitoring during treatment.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ Informs the patient of the follow-up appointment schedule and any notes for tests needed at the next visit (if applicable).<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ The doctor issues a follow-up appointment slip clearly stating the date and time. For BHYT patients, the doctor selects the appropriate record code in the examination software matching the diagnosis and the diagnosis on the patient's transfer letter (per HD.25.01).<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ The doctor states the reason and signs next to any case requiring transfer to Day-Treatment or Emergency. The Day-Treatment or Emergency Department is then responsible for management, explaining the treatment direction, prescribing, or admitting the patient depending on their condition.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ The doctor explains the condition and prints an admission form if the patient needs to be hospitalized.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ The doctor explains the condition to the patient, seeks the Board of Directors' approval, and prints a hospital-transfer form if the patient needs to be referred to another facility per regulations.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ For patients admitted under direct-access insurance coverage, the admitting doctor is responsible for deciding which tests are insurance-covered after accounting reclassifies the patient category. |
| **Nurse at the follow-up appointment desk** | Stamp the outpatient record or chronic-disease management program (if applicable). Guide the patient through the necessary procedures for the next visit | The nurse checks the prescription: correct medication quantities, correct dates. Service (self-pay) patients are guided to their follow-up visit and to purchase medication at the hospital pharmacy. For BHYT prescriptions, staff stamp "BHYT Verified" at the bottom of each prescription so accounting staff know the patient has passed through the follow-up appointment desk. If this stamp is missing, accounting staff ask the patient to return to the follow-up desk for the stamp before approving the prescription.<br>- For BHYT patients without an existing outpatient record:<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ If the patient does not qualify for the year-round BHYT program: stamp a second follow-up appointment and the transfer date onto the follow-up slip.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ If the patient qualifies for the year-round BHYT program, stamp the appropriate program onto the follow-up slip and inform the patient a new transfer letter is not required.<br>- For BHYT patients with an existing outpatient record:<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ If the outpatient record shows a second visit, guide the patient to obtain a new transfer letter for their next follow-up visit.<br>&nbsp;&nbsp;+ If the record is already under a program, continue stamping the existing program onto the follow-up slip.<br>=> After passing through the follow-up desk, BHYT patients are asked to return to the payment counter to have their insurance approved and sign the necessary documents to complete the examination process.<br>* If the patient requires hospital admission, guide and process admission per the inpatient admission procedure (QT.25.04).<br>* Process referral transfers, issue sick-leave certificates, and contact doctors for cases requiring a consultation opinion. |
| **Administrative desk nurse** | — | - If the patient requires hospital admission, guide and process admission per the inpatient admission procedure (QT.25.04).<br>- Process referral transfers and issue sick-leave certificates as requested by the examining doctor.<br>- Guide and process other administrative procedures as required by professional needs and department task assignments. |
| **Accounting** | Approve insurance prescriptions and collect any price difference (if applicable). Collect payment for service (self-pay) prescriptions | Accounting collects the co-payment amount according to the patient's BHYT coverage level and any difference versus the insurance prescription (if applicable). Patients purchasing self-pay medication pay for it to accounting at the service pharmacy counter. Accounting staff stamp the prescription as paid and direct the patient to collect BHYT medication or purchase service medication. |
| **Service pharmacy / Insurance pharmacy counter** | Patient collects medication (BHYT) or purchases medication -> end of the examination process | - The patient signs the prescription.<br>- Pharmacy counter staff check the prescription: quantity matches dosage and the follow-up appointment date.<br>- Pharmacy department staff approve the dispensing of insurance-covered medication for BHYT prescriptions.<br>- Medication is dispensed per the prescription.<br>- The prescription is stamped as dispensed.<br>=> The patient's examination process is complete and they leave the hospital. |
